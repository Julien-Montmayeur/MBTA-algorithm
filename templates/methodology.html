Methodology

How the Subway Expansion Algorithm Works

Scoring Formula

Each potential project is assigned a score based on the following weighted factors:

  • Transit Dependence – 30%
  • Distance from Subway – 20%
  • Income Level (Inverted) – 15%
  • Population Growth Rate – 15%
  • Construction Feasibility – 10%
  • Distance from Downtown – 10%

Scenarios

The algorithm allows users to choose a planning scenario, which changes how the weights are applied:

  • Climate Focus: Prioritizes transit dependence, feasibility, and low income areas.
  • Cost Saving: Emphasizes feasibility and low-cost surface or upgrade modes.
  • Growth Focus: Focuses on population growth and long-term expansion needs.

Optimization Logic

The algorithm runs a greedy budget-constrained selection: it ranks all projects by score-to-cost ratio and picks the highest-value projects until the budget is used up. This ensures maximum impact per dollar.

Extra Features

  • Project Justifications: Each selected project has a one-line rationale generated based on score criteria.
  • Gap Index: Calculates how close unselected neighborhoods came to qualifying.
  • Scenario Comparison: Runs all three scenarios and compares total score and project overlap.
